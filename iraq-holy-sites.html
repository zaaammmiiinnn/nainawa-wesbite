The Sacred Sites of Iraq

Journey through the Cradle of Shia Islam

Iraq, the land of prophets and Imams, houses the most sacred sites in Shia Islam. From the holy cities of Najaf and Karbala to Samarra and Baghdad, each site resonates with profound spiritual significance.

"قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ (ص): لَيَأْتِيَنَّ عَلَى النَّاسِ زَمَانٌ لَتَغْدُو الرَّاحَةُ فِيهِ إِلَى الْعِرَاقِ"

"The Prophet (pbuh) said: There will come a time when comfort will be found in Iraq."

Major Holy Cities

Najaf

Home to the shrine of Imam Ali (a.s.), the first Shia Imam. The Wadi-us-Salaam cemetery, the largest in the world, surrounds the shrine.

  • Shrine of Imam Ali (a.s.) with its golden dome
  • Hawza Ilmiyya - Center of Shia scholarship
  • Cave of Hira recreation site
  • Masjid al-Hannana with the "Weeping Pillar"

Karbala

Site of Imam Hussain's (a.s.) martyrdom. The twin shrines of Imam Hussain and Abbas (a.s.) attract millions annually.

  • Shrine of Imam Hussain (a.s.)
  • Al-Abbas Shrine
  • Qatrah Desert - Site of Imam Hussain's camp
  • Al-Hannana Mosque

Samarra

Contains the shrines of the 10th and 11th Imams, Ali al-Hadi (a.s.) and Hasan al-Askari (a.s.).

  • Al-Askari Shrine with its golden dome
  • Sardab - Hideout of Imam Mahdi (a.j.)
  • Malwiya Minaret - Ancient spiral tower

Important Dates

Annual Events

  • Arbaeen Pilgrimage

    20 Safar - World's largest annual gathering (20-30 million pilgrims)

  • Ashura

    10 Muharram - Commemoration of Imam Hussain's martyrdom

  • 21 Ramadan

    Martyrdom of Imam Ali (a.s.) - Night vigils in Najaf

Birth Anniversaries

  • 15 Sha'ban

    Birth of Imam Mahdi (a.j.) celebrated in Samarra

  • 13 Rajab

    Birth of Imam Ali (a.s.) - Celebrations in Najaf

  • 3 Sha'ban

    Birth of Imam Hussain (a.s.) - Karbala celebrations

Pilgrimage Guide

Essential Practices

  • Walk barefoot between Najaf and Karbala during Arbaeen
  • Recite Ziyarat Ashura at Imam Hussain's shrine
  • Perform 2 rak'at prayer in Masjid al-Hannana
  • Visit Wadi-us-Salaam cemetery at night

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October-April (cooler months)
  • Dress conservatively - abaya/chador mandatory for women
  • Use official pilgrimage buses between cities
  • Carry pilgrimage guidebooks in Arabic/English

Planning Your Pilgrimage

Best times to visit are during spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) for pleasant weather
Ensure you have proper visas and travel documents for Iran
Learn basic Farsi phrases or travel with a guide who can translate
Prepare by learning the specific ziyarat prayers for each shrine
