Etiquette for Visiting Holy Shrines

Guidelines for a respectful and spiritually fulfilling pilgrimage

Visiting the holy shrines of the Ahlulbayt (a.s.) is a profound spiritual experience that requires proper preparation and etiquette (adab). These guidelines will help ensure your pilgrimage is both respectful and spiritually rewarding.

"إنما يعمر مساجد الله من آمن بالله واليوم الآخر"

"The mosques of Allah shall be maintained only by those who believe in Allah and the Last Day." (Quran 9:18)

Dress Code

For Men

  • Wear modest, clean clothing that covers from navel to knees at minimum
  • Shirts should cover shoulders (no tank tops or sleeveless shirts)
  • Avoid tight or revealing clothing
  • Remove shoes before entering shrine areas (follow local customs)
  • Head covering is recommended but not required in most shrines

For Women

  • Full hijab (headscarf and loose clothing covering entire body except face and hands)
  • Abaya or chador is required in many shrines (available for purchase/rent locally)
  • Avoid perfumes or scented products
  • Wear comfortable, slip-on shoes for easy removal
  • Some shrines have separate entrances and sections for women

Behavior Inside Shrines

Spiritual Conduct

  • Maintain a state of wudhu (ablution) when possible
  • Enter with right foot first while reciting the du'a for entering a shrine(s)
  • Walk calmly and avoid rushing, especially near the zarih (shrine enclosure)
  • Keep voice low - many pilgrims are engaged in prayer and meditation
  • Mobile phones should be silenced

Prohibited Actions

  • No photography in restricted areas (observe posted signs)
  • Do not touch or lean on the zarih unless permitted
  • Avoid turning your back to the shrine when leaving
  • No eating or drinking inside the shrine area
  • Do not disturb other pilgrims' worship or privacy

Ziyarat Rituals

Before Entering

  • Perform ghusl (ritual bath) if possible, or at least wudhu
  • Recite the intention (niyyah) for ziyarat
  • Give sadaqah (charity) before entering if possible

At the Shrine

  • Recite the specific ziyarat for that Imam (available in booklets or apps)
  • Offer two rak'at salat as a gift to the Imam
  • Make personal duas at the head or foot of the shrine (depending on local customs)
  • Touch or kiss the zarih if permitted (observe local customs)

After Ziyarat

  • Recite the dua for leaving the shrine
  • Exit with left foot first
  • Consider giving additional sadaqah in gratitude
  • Maintain the spiritual state by avoiding idle talk immediately after

Cultural Considerations

Local Customs

  • Each shrine may have slightly different customs - observe and follow locals
  • In Iraq, men often place their forehead on the zarih during dua
  • In Iran, pilgrims may stand at certain distances from the zarih
  • Some shrines have specific times for women-only access
  • Follow instructions from shrine guards and volunteers

Photography

  • Always ask permission before photographing people
  • Some shrines prohibit photography of the zarih - observe signs
  • Never photograph people in vulnerable states of worship
  • Turn off flash out of respect
  • Consider experiencing moments without the camera

Special Circumstances

During Peak Times

  • Arbaeen and other significant dates bring large crowds - be patient
  • Follow the flow of movement around the shrine
  • Consider visiting during less crowded times if possible
  • Keep personal items secure in crowded conditions

For Elderly or Disabled

  • Most shrines have special access routes - ask guards for assistance
  • Wheelchairs are often available at major shrines
  • Consider visiting during less crowded times for easier access
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help from shrine volunteers

With Children

  • Prepare children beforehand about proper behavior
  • Dress children modestly according to shrine standards
  • Hold hands in crowded areas
  • Brief visits may be better for young children

Essential Reminders

Approach the shrine with love and reverence in your heart
Be mindful of your intentions throughout the visit
Respect the diversity of pilgrims' expressions of devotion
Allow ample time - rushing diminishes the spiritual experience
