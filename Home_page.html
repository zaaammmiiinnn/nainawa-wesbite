About Ziyarat Tours

Founded in 2012 by a group of religious scholars and travel experts, Nainawa Ziyarat Tours has grown to become one of the most trusted names in Shia pilgrimage travel. Our team consists of experienced guides, multilingual staff, and religious scholars who ensure your journey is both spiritually fulfilling and logistically seamless.

  • Endorsed by Ayatollah Sistani's office in Najaf

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about pilgrimage travel with Ziyarat Tours.

You will need:

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Visa for Iraq/Iran (we assist with processing)
  • COVID vaccination certificate if required
  • Travel insurance (we can arrange this for you)
  • 2 passport-sized photos

Yes, the holy cities are generally safe for pilgrims. We take several precautions:

  • All our transportation is with trusted, licensed drivers
  • We stay in secure hotels with 24/7 security
  • Our local guides are familiar with safe routes and areas
  • We monitor security situations and adjust itineraries if needed
  • Emergency contact numbers are provided 24/7 during your trip

Modest Islamic dress is required at all holy sites:

  • Men: Long pants, shirts covering shoulders (no shorts or sleeveless)
  • Women: Full-length abaya/chador (available for purchase locally), headscarf covering hair
  • Comfortable walking shoes as there's lots of walking
  • Layered clothing as temperatures vary
  • Avoid flashy jewelry or expensive items

We provide detailed packing lists after booking.

Absolutely! We regularly accommodate elderly pilgrims and those with mobility challenges:

  • Wheelchair assistance available at airports and shrines
  • Hotels with elevator access
  • Option to use taxis instead of walking when possible
  • Doctor travels with larger groups
  • Slower-paced itineraries available
  • Special dietary needs accommodated

Please inform us of any special requirements when booking.

  • All accommodation (double occupancy, single supplement available)
  • All transportation during the pilgrimage
  • Visa processing assistance
  • Breakfast and dinner daily
  • Religious guide and scholar accompaniment
  • Entrance fees to all shrines and historical sites
  • Basic medical assistance

Not included: International flights, travel insurance, lunches, personal expenses, tips.
